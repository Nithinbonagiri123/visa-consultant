The student visa is not a single application — it's a sequence of about a dozen smaller documents, each with a deadline, each easy to get wrong. Here's the full sequence for an Indian student, regardless of country.

## The 8-step student visa roadmap

### 1. Confirm your university offer

You can't apply for a visa without an unconditional offer letter. If you have a conditional offer (e.g. "subject to IELTS 6.5"), clear the conditions first. Most consulates won't process a visa without the unconditional version.

### 2. Pay the tuition deposit (if required)

For some countries you need to confirm enrolment with a deposit before visa application:
- **USA** — pay the SEVIS fee (US$350) and university confirmation deposit (varies by school).
- **Canada** — fully prepay the first year of tuition is required for SDS.
- **Australia** — pay the OSHC + Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE) fee.
- **UK / Ireland / Germany / France** — partial or no deposit usually required.

### 3. Arrange financial proof

Each country wants different proof, but the principle is the same: **show that you can cover tuition + first-year living without working.**

- **USA** — bank statements or affidavit of support covering 1 year on the I-20.
- **Canada (SDS)** — GIC of CAD 20,635 + first-year tuition paid.
- **UK** — £1,334/month for 9 months (London) or £1,023/month (elsewhere), held for 28 days.
- **Germany** — €11,904 in a blocked account (Sperrkonto).
- **Australia** — AUD 29,710 + tuition + return airfare.
- **France** — €615/month minimum living + tuition.
- **Ireland** — €10,000 in your bank account + tuition.

### 4. Apply on the consulate portal

Each country has its own portal:
- **USA** — DS-160 + interview slot at the US embassy/consulate.
- **Canada** — IRCC portal (online).
- **UK** — UKVI portal + biometrics at VFS Global.
- **Australia** — ImmiAccount portal.
- **Schengen (France/Germany/Italy)** — VFS Global or country-specific service provider.

### 5. Submit documents

Standard document set:
- Passport (valid 6+ months beyond planned return)
- Unconditional offer letter
- Tuition fee receipt (if paid)
- Financial proof
- English test scorecard
- Academic transcripts (10th, 12th, bachelor's)
- Statement of Purpose (SOP)
- Reference letters (LORs)
- Visa application form + fee receipt
- Passport-size photographs (country-specific size)

### 6. Biometrics + interview

Most countries require biometric data (fingerprints + photo) at a VFS centre. The USA also requires a consular interview. The UK and Canada usually don't, though Canada occasionally calls students in for interviews if SOPs are weak.

### 7. Wait for the decision

| Country     | Typical processing time           |
|-------------|------------------------------------|
| USA         | 2–6 weeks after consular interview |
| Canada      | 8–12 weeks (SDS expedites to 3–4)  |
| UK          | 3 weeks standard, 5 days priority  |
| Australia   | 4–6 weeks                          |
| Ireland     | 4–8 weeks                          |
| Germany     | 6–12 weeks                         |
| France      | 4–8 weeks                          |
| New Zealand | 4–6 weeks                          |
| UAE         | 2–4 weeks                          |

### 8. Pre-departure prep

After visa approval:
- Confirm flight + accommodation
- Buy forex (50–60% in card, rest in cash)
- Activate international SIM
- Print all documents (offer, visa, financials)
- Carry one set of original certificates + one photocopy

## The four mistakes that get most rejections

1. **Weak SOP** — generic, doesn't tie the programme to clear career goals, or doesn't justify country choice.
2. **Inconsistent financial proof** — sudden large deposits without a trail trigger suspicion.
3. **Wrong English test score** — applying with IELTS 6.0 to a country requiring 6.5 in each module.
4. **Vague intent to return** — saying "I will work and stay" when the visa requires demonstrating ties to India.

## The country-specific quirks

- **USA F-1** — the I-20 must match your funding documents exactly. Even ₹500 difference triggers a 221(g) administrative processing hold.
- **Canada SDS** — you must apply *as an Indian citizen* to qualify, and the GIC must be from a designated bank.
- **UK** — Tuberculosis test certificate required at the medical examination centre approved by the British high commission.
- **Germany** — APS certificate from the German embassy in New Delhi is mandatory for most courses.
- **France** — the Campus France interview is informal but mandatory and influences the visa decision.
