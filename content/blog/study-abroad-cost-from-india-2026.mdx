## The costs no one mentions

These add ₹3–8L per year to your real budget. Plan for them.

- **Application fees** — ₹3,000–₹15,000 per university. With 6 applications, ₹30K–₹90K.
- **English test** — IELTS ₹17K (retakes are common), PTE ₹17K, TOEFL ₹15K.
- **GRE / GMAT** — ₹17K–₹25K for STEM/MBA destinations.
- **Visa fees + biometrics** — ₹15K–₹50K depending on country.
- **One-way flight** — ₹50K–₹1.2L depending on country and season.
- **First-month setup** — accommodation deposit, phone SIM, transit pass, basic furniture: ₹1.5L–₹3L.
- **Health insurance** — ₹35K–₹2.5L per year depending on country.
- **Currency conversion buffer** — keep 5–10% extra for the falling rupee.

## How Indian students actually fund this

Most students use a mix of three or four of the below:

1. **Family savings** — usually 30–60% of the total.
2. **Education loan (collateral)** — up to ₹1.5 crore at 9–11% interest from PSU banks; covers up to 90% of cost.
3. **Education loan (non-collateral)** — up to ₹40L from private banks and NBFCs at 11–14%.
4. **Scholarships** — typically 10–50% tuition waivers from the university, plus government scholarships (Chevening, Fulbright-Nehru, DAAD).
5. **Teaching / research assistantships** — common in US PhD/master's programmes; can cover full tuition + stipend.
6. **Part-time work** — 20 hours/week during term in most countries; ₹3–8L/year contribution to living costs.

## The cheapest realistic budgets

- **Germany public university** — ₹18–25L for a two-year master's, all-in.
- **France public university** — ₹22–30L for a two-year master's.
- **UAE branch campus** — ₹35–50L for a two-year master's.
- **Canada with PGWP factored in** — break-even by year 3 of work for most STEM graduates.

## The hidden ROI factor

Most students focus on cost. The bigger lever is **stay-back income**:

- A Canadian or Australian STEM graduate earning ₹40–60L/year for 3–4 years post-graduation recoups even a ₹70L investment.
- A UK Graduate Route worker on a £35K salary recoups a ₹50L investment in roughly 2.5 years.
- A US OPT engineer at US$95K starting recoups ₹70L within 1.5 years.
