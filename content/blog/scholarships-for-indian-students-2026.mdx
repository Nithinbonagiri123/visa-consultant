The "fully funded scholarship for Indian students" market is more competitive than ever, but real money is still on the table. Here's what's actually available in 2026, broken down by what's realistic to win.

## The top fully-funded scholarships (tuition + stipend)

These are the marquee, government-funded scholarships. Selective, prestigious, and worth the effort to apply.

### Chevening (UK)
- **Coverage:** Full tuition + £1,300/month stipend + travel + visa
- **For:** Master's in the UK
- **Realistic odds:** Highly competitive — 1,500 awarded globally each year
- **Application opens:** August, closes October–November

### Fulbright-Nehru (USA)
- **Coverage:** Full tuition + stipend + travel + health insurance
- **For:** Master's and PhD in the USA
- **Realistic odds:** Highly competitive — under 100 awarded annually
- **Application opens:** Spring, closes mid-year

### Commonwealth Scholarships (UK)
- **Coverage:** Full tuition + stipend + travel
- **For:** Master's and PhD in the UK
- **Realistic odds:** Competitive — about 200 awarded per year to Indians

### DAAD (Germany)
- **Coverage:** €861–€1,300/month stipend + travel + health insurance + tuition
- **For:** Master's and PhD in Germany
- **Realistic odds:** Competitive — well-supported for STEM students

### Australia Awards
- **Coverage:** Full tuition + stipend + travel + health
- **For:** Master's and PhD in Australia
- **Realistic odds:** Highly selective; tied to developmental priorities

### Vanier Canada Graduate Scholarships
- **Coverage:** CAD 50,000/year for 3 years
- **For:** Doctoral students only
- **Realistic odds:** Highly competitive — about 166 per year globally

### Eiffel Excellence (France)
- **Coverage:** €1,181/month + tuition + travel + insurance
- **For:** Master's and PhD in France
- **Realistic odds:** Moderately competitive

### Manaaki Scholarships (New Zealand)
- **Coverage:** Full tuition + stipend + travel
- **For:** Master's and PhD in New Zealand
- **Realistic odds:** Selective; specific programme focus

## Partial scholarships — the easier wins

This is where most students actually find funding.

### University merit scholarships (everywhere)
Most universities offer 10–50% tuition waivers based on academic merit. **Apply automatically** to most schools — no separate application.

### Country-specific awards
- **Charpak Scholarship (France)** — government of France for Indian students; €5,000–€7,000.
- **Government of Ireland International Scholarships** — €10,000 with full fee waiver for one year.
- **Destination Australia** — AUD 15,000/year for studying at a regional Australian university.
- **New Zealand Excellence Awards** — NZD 5,000–10,000 for Indian students at NZ universities.
- **Erasmus Mundus Joint Master's** — fully-funded multi-country master's across EU.

### Need-based US aid
Need-blind US universities (Harvard, MIT, Princeton, Yale) commit to meeting full demonstrated financial need for international students. Not a scholarship in the traditional sense, but powerful for high-academic-low-income profiles.

## Field-specific scholarships

- **Women in STEM** — Schlumberger Foundation, Google Women Techmakers, Adobe Research Women in Technology.
- **Engineering** — Boeing, Siemens, ABB campus scholarships at partner universities.
- **Healthcare / nursing** — many countries offer skills-shortage scholarships (Australia, NZ, Canada).
- **Climate / sustainability** — increasing number of awards from Schwarzman, Knight-Hennessy.

## How Indian students actually win scholarships

1. **Start early.** Many scholarships close 8–10 months before the intake.
2. **Apply for 8–12 scholarships in parallel** — not one or two.
3. **Tailor the essay every time.** Generic SOPs lose. Specific career goals tied to the scholarship's mission win.
4. **Strong recommenders.** Two strong LORs beat five lukewarm ones.
5. **Quantify everything.** Marks, project outcomes, internship impact — in numbers.
6. **Apply to schools with automatic merit aid.** It's the highest-conversion funding source.

## The scholarship myth

A common misconception: "I'll get a full scholarship and won't need to pay." For 99% of students, this isn't realistic. The realistic outcome is:
- **20–40% tuition waiver** from the university (very common with strong profiles).
- **Plus** a country-specific or external scholarship of ₹3–10L (achievable with effort).
- **Combined with** an education loan and family contribution for the rest.

This combo can bring a ₹40L total cost down to ₹20–25L out of pocket.
