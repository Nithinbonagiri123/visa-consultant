The students who succeed at studying abroad are not the smartest — they're the ones who started 12 months early and worked a clear plan. Here's that plan, working backwards from your target intake.

## The 12-month roadmap

### 12 months before intake — *foundation*

- **Decide your country.** Use the [Study Destination Finder](/find-your-destination) or talk to a counsellor.
- **Identify your field** and 3–4 specific master's programmes that match.
- **Take a diagnostic IELTS / PTE / GRE / GMAT.** Know your starting band.
- **Map your finances.** Rough budget, family contribution, loan capacity.

### 10–11 months — *test prep*

- **Begin focused English-test prep.** Aim for the band one tier above the country requirement.
- **If applying to the US, start GRE / GMAT prep.**
- **Update your CV** — academic projects, internships, publications, awards.

### 8–9 months — *applications*

- **Build a final university list** — 1 ambitious + 2 target + 1 safe.
- **Draft your Statement of Purpose** — one for each university, customised.
- **Request LORs** from 2–3 professors / managers.
- **Take your English test** if not already cleared.

### 6–7 months — *submit*

- **Submit applications** to all shortlisted universities.
- **Apply for scholarships** in parallel with applications.
- **Pre-approve your education loan** with a PSU bank or NBFC.

### 4–5 months — *decisions + commit*

- **Wait for admits** (most universities respond in 4–8 weeks).
- **Compare offers** — total cost, scholarship, programme fit, location.
- **Pay the confirmation deposit** to your chosen university.

### 2–3 months — *visa*

- **Arrange financial proof** (bank statements, blocked account, GIC, etc.).
- **Book biometrics + visa interview slot.**
- **Prepare your visa file** — every document, every photocopy.
- **Submit visa application.**

### 1 month — *pre-departure*

- **Book your flight** (one-way, cheaper midweek).
- **Buy forex** (split: 50% card, 30% cash, 20% in your account).
- **Secure accommodation** — university housing or private rental.
- **Get medical check-up** and required vaccinations.
- **Activate international SIM.**
- **Print every document** (visa, offer, financials, accommodation).

## The five non-obvious things that make or break the plan

### 1. Start IELTS prep too late

Most students underestimate this. A 6.0 to 7.0 jump can take 8–12 weeks of focused effort. Start at month 10–11, not month 6.

### 2. Apply to too many universities

Six applications, well-researched, beat fifteen scattered ones. Quality of SOP and university fit matter more than quantity.

### 3. Underestimate the loan timeline

PSU banks take 6–10 weeks to disburse. NBFCs take 4–6. Start the loan process at month 5–6, not month 2.

### 4. Ignore the SOP

The SOP is what makes universities accept or reject borderline students. Treat it like a 3-month project, not a weekend essay.

### 5. Skip pre-departure prep

The first two weeks abroad are when most international students get overwhelmed. A 5-day pre-departure session — accommodation, banking, transit, culture — flattens the learning curve dramatically.

## The four major intakes

- **September / Fall** — biggest intake; most programmes available.
- **January / Spring** — second-biggest; common in UK, Ireland, US.
- **May / Summer** — small intake; mostly Canada and a few US programmes.
- **February / July** — Australia and New Zealand main intakes.

## What "studying abroad" actually demands

It's not a one-day decision. It's a 9–12 month project with:
- 100+ hours of test prep
- 30–60 hours of essay writing
- 6–12 weeks of paperwork
- 6–10 weeks of waiting

The students we see thrive abroad are the ones who took the planning seriously, picked one country and committed early, and built a clear plan with deadlines.
